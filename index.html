An AI layer for hospital work

Hospital work happens between the systems.

Patient information lives in dozens of programs that don't talk to each other. Every day, people carry it across the gaps by hand. We're building an AI layer that understands what hospitals already know — and carries the routine work in between.

The idea

Hospitals don't lack information — they lack access to it in the moment it matters. What a patient needs is often already recorded somewhere; someone just has to go and find it, re-type it, and pass it on.

Modern AI can finally read and connect what's spread across a hospital's systems. Onsano is building that layer: software that brings together what the systems already contain, prepares the routine work that follows from it, and leaves every decision about care with the people trained to make it.

How we build

  • Judgment stays human

    Software can gather, sort, and prepare. Decisions about care belong to clinicians — that line doesn't move.

  • Start from what exists

    Hospitals shouldn't need to replace their systems to benefit from AI. We build on the infrastructure that's already in place.

  • European by design

    Data protection, security, and accountability are ground rules, not features. We build for the realities of European healthcare.

Where we are

Onsano is at the very beginning. We're spending our time inside hospitals, learning how the work actually gets done, before deciding what to build first.
